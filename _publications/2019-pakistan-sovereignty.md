This article critically examines the tension between Pakistan's constitutionally enshrined state sovereignty and the overt political and economic dictation exercised by Saudi Arabia. Drawing on foreign policy analysis frameworks, it explores how financial dependency and strategic alignment have at times constrained Pakistan's autonomous decision-making at the international level.

## Abstract

This M.Phil. thesis examines the evolving Pakistan-Turkey relationship through the lens of soft power theory. Traditionally anchored in security and strategic interests, the bilateral relationship underwent a significant paradigm shift following two catalytic events: Pakistan's placement on the FATF grey list in 2018, and the erosion of Turkey's soft power influence due to the Syrian crisis.

Drawing on Joseph Nye's soft power framework, the thesis argues that both nations have strategically pivoted toward cultural, educational, and diplomatic cooperation as tools to rehabilitate their international image. The research dissects the historical and contemporary dimensions of Pakistan-Turkey mutual cooperation, illuminates the soft power avenues being pursued, and identifies the formulation and implementation challenges both countries face in sustaining this positive trajectory.

## Key Findings

- Pakistan and Turkey share complementary soft power deficits that have driven closer bilateral cooperation
- Cultural diplomacy, media exchange, and educational partnerships are the primary soft power instruments in use
- Both countries face structural challenges — domestic political instability and institutional capacity — that limit soft power effectiveness
- A coordinated soft power strategy could yield significant gains in international standing for both nations
